\section*{\centering \Large{\textbf{\MakeUppercase{Abstract}} }}
    An abstract should be a concise summary of a research paper or project, written as a single paragraph. It typically includes the purpose of the study, the methods used, key results, and the main conclusion. The goal is to give readers a quick overview of the work so they can decide whether to read the full document. It should be clear, specific, and limited to around 150–250 words, avoiding unnecessary details or citations. Writing in the past tense and maintaining an objective tone is recommended.
